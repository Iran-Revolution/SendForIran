# Iran Protests Research - France
**Last Updated: January 12, 2026**

## Current Situation Overview

Since **December 28, 2025**, massive protests have erupted across Iran sparked by a severe economic crisis:

- **Death Toll**: 540+ protesters killed (as of January 12, 2026)
- **Detained**: Over 10,600 people arrested
- **Trigger**: Iranian rial collapsed to all-time low; severe inflation
- **Internet Blackout**: Nationwide blackout imposed on January 8, 2026

## Political Reactions

### President Emmanuel Macron
- **January 9, 2026**: Joint statement with UK and Germany: *"We are deeply concerned about reports of violence by Iranian security forces."*
- *"We strongly condemn the killing of protestors"*
- Called on Iranian authorities to protect peaceful assembly
- Previously expressed concern over reports of protesters killed in Iran

### Joint France-UK-Germany Statement (January 9, 2026)
Leaders' Declaration:
- *"Deeply concerned about reports of violence by Iranian security forces"*
- *"Strongly condemn the killing of protestors"*
- Urged Iranian authorities to respect rights of peaceful protesters
- Coordinated European response

### French Foreign Ministry
- France expressed concern over reports of protesters killed
- Monitoring situation closely through E3 format

### European Union (French participation)
- EU ready to impose further sanctions on Iran
- European External Action Service condemned violence
- EU High Representative Kaja Kallas denounced "disproportionate" response
