# Iran Protests Research - United Kingdom
**Last Updated: January 12, 2026**

## Current Situation Overview

Since **December 28, 2025**, massive protests have erupted across Iran sparked by a severe economic crisis:

- **Death Toll**: 540+ protesters killed (as of January 12, 2026)
- **Detained**: Over 10,600 people arrested
- **Trigger**: Iranian rial collapsed to all-time low; severe inflation
- **Internet Blackout**: Nationwide blackout imposed on January 8, 2026

## Political Reactions

### Prime Minister Keir Starmer
- **January 9, 2026**: Joint statement with France and Germany: *"We are deeply concerned about reports of violence by Iranian security forces... We strongly condemn the killing of protestors."*
- Called on Iranian authorities to protect peaceful assembly

### Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO)
- Issued travel advisory: **"Against all travel to Iran"**
- Warned UK government unable to evacuate British nationals or provide face-to-face consular assistance

### Joint UK-France-Germany Statement (January 9, 2026)
Leaders' Declaration:
- *"Deeply concerned about reports of violence by Iranian security forces"*
- *"Strongly condemn the killing of protestors"*
- Called on Iranian authorities to respect rights of peaceful protesters

### UK Parliament
- Hansard debates on Middle East situation
- Cross-party concern over Iranian regime's violent crackdown
