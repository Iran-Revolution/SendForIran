# Iran Protests Research - United States
**Last Updated: January 12, 2026**

## Current Situation Overview

Since **December 28, 2025**, massive protests have erupted across Iran sparked by a severe economic crisis:

- **Death Toll**: 540+ protesters killed (as of January 12, 2026)
- **Detained**: Over 10,600 people arrested
- **Trigger**: Iranian rial plunged to all-time low against USD; severe inflation and economic collapse
- **Internet Blackout**: Nationwide communications blackout imposed on January 8, 2026

## Political Reactions

### President Donald Trump
- **January 2, 2026**: Warned the US would intervene if Iran violently suppresses protests: *"If Iran shoots and violently kills peaceful protesters, the United States of America will come to their rescue. We are locked and loaded and ready to go."*
- **January 4, 2026**: Stated Iran would be *"hit very hard"* if security forces continued killing demonstrators
- **January 9, 2026**: Issued social media warning: *"The eyes of the world are upon you. Take to the streets."*

### Secretary of State Marco Rubio
- **January 10, 2026**: *"The United States supports the brave people of Iran"*
- Called on Iranian authorities to respect citizens' rights

### U.S. State Department
- **January 5, 2026**: Posted warning in Persian on X: *"President Trump is a man of action. If you didn't know before, now you do. Don't mess with President Trump."*
- **January 6, 2026**: Called hospital attacks on injured protesters *"a clear crime against humanity"*

### Senator Lindsey Graham
- **January 7, 2026**: *"To the Ayatollahs: you need to understand—if you keep killing your people who are demanding a better life—Donald J. Trump is going to kill you."*
- Said *"change is coming to Iran"* and called for the end of the *"Nazi regime"*

### Senator Ted Cruz
- **January 7, 2026**: Supported protests against the *"tyrannical"* and *"theocratic"* regime
- *"The people of Iran want to stand with America. They want to stand with freedom."*

### UN Ambassador Michael Waltz
- *"The people of Iran want freedom. They have suffered at the hands of the ayatollahs for too long. We stand with Iranians in the streets of Tehran."*
