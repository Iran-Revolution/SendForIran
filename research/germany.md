# Iran Protests Research - Germany

## Current Situation Overview

Since **December 28, 2025**, massive protests have erupted across Iran sparked by a severe economic crisis:

- **Death Toll**: 540+ protesters killed (as of January 12, 2026)
- **Detained**: Over 10,600 people arrested
- **Trigger**: Iranian rial collapsed to all-time low; severe inflation
- **Internet Blackout**: Nationwide blackout imposed on January 8, 2026

## Political Reactions

### Chancellor Friedrich Merz
- **January 9, 2026**: Joint statement with UK and France: *"We are deeply concerned about reports of violence by Iranian security forces."*
- *"We strongly condemn the killing of protestors"*
- Called on Iranian authorities to protect peaceful assembly

### Foreign Minister Johann Wadephul
- **January 8, 2026**: Condemned *"excessive use of force"* against demonstrators in Iran
- Criticized security forces for violence against protesters
- Posted on X condemning the crackdown

### Joint Germany-France-UK Statement (January 9, 2026)
- Leaders' Declaration of France, the United Kingdom and Germany on the situation in Iran
- *"Deeply concerned about reports of violence by Iranian security forces"*
- *"Strongly condemn the killing of protestors"*
- Urged Iranian authorities to respect rights of peaceful protesters

### European Union Position
- EU ready to propose tougher sanctions on Iran over protest repression
- European External Action Service called on Iran to uphold freedom of expression and peaceful assembly
- EU High Representative Kaja Kallas denounced "disproportionate" response to protests
